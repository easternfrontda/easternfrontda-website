# Remove Black Background from Logo - Photopea Tutorial

## **Step-by-Step Guide to Make Your Logo Transparent**

---

## **STEP 1: Open Photopea**
1. Go to: **https://www.photopea.com/**
2. Wait for it to load (looks like Photoshop)
3. You'll see a blank canvas

---

## **STEP 2: Open Your Logo**
1. Click **"File"** (top left corner)
2. Click **"Open"**
3. **Download your logo first** from: https://www.genspark.ai/api/files/s/8rsTMld9
4. Select your downloaded logo file
5. Click **"Open"**
6. Your logo will appear on the canvas

---

## **STEP 3: Add Transparency Support**
1. Look at the **right side panel** - you'll see "Layers"
2. You should see a layer called **"Background"** with a little lock icon 🔒
3. **Right-click** on that "Background" layer
4. Select **"Layer from Background"** from the menu
5. A dialog box will appear - just click **"OK"**
6. The layer is now called "Layer 0" and the lock is gone ✅

---

## **STEP 4: Select the Black Background**
1. Look at the **left toolbar**
2. Find the **Magic Wand Tool** 🪄 (looks like a magic wand)
   - It might be hidden under the "Quick Selection Tool"
   - If you see a dotted rectangle, **click and HOLD** on it to reveal other selection tools
   - Select **"Magic Wand Tool"**
3. At the **top toolbar**, set these options:
   - **Tolerance:** 30-40 (this determines how similar colors it selects)
   - Make sure **"Contiguous"** is UNCHECKED ✅ (this will select ALL black areas at once)
4. Click on the **black background** anywhere
5. You should see "marching ants" (animated dotted lines) around all the black areas

---

## **STEP 5: Delete the Black Background**
1. Press the **Delete** key on your keyboard
2. The black background should disappear!
3. You'll see a **checkerboard pattern** - that means it's transparent! ✅
4. Press **Ctrl+D** (or Cmd+D on Mac) to deselect

---

## **STEP 6: Clean Up Any Remaining Black**
If you still see some black edges or areas:
1. Use the **Magic Wand Tool** again
2. Click on any remaining black spots
3. Press **Delete**
4. Repeat until all black is gone
5. Press **Ctrl+D** to deselect when done

**Pro Tip:** If the magic wand selects too much or too little:
- Increase **Tolerance** to select more similar colors (try 50-60)
- Decrease **Tolerance** to be more precise (try 20-30)

---

## **STEP 7: Export as Transparent PNG**
1. Click **"File"** (top left)
2. Click **"Export As"**
3. Select **"PNG"**
4. A dialog will appear with export options:
   - Make sure **"Transparent background"** is selected ✅
   - Quality: Leave at default (or set to 100%)
5. Click **"Save"**
6. Choose where to save it on your computer
7. Name it something like: **"EFDA-Logo-Transparent.png"**
8. Click **"Save"**

---

## **STEP 8: Test Your Transparent Logo**
1. Find the file you just saved
2. Open it in **Windows Photo Viewer** or **Preview (Mac)**
3. You should see the shield and text with NO black background
4. The background should appear white or checkered (depending on your viewer)

---

## **STEP 9: Use in VistaPrint**
Now you can upload this transparent PNG to VistaPrint and it will look perfect on any background color!

## **Troubleshooting:**

### **Problem:** "Contiguous" option is grayed out
**Solution:** You're using the wrong tool. Make sure you selected the **Magic Wand Tool**, not the Quick Selection Tool.

### **Problem:** Only part of the black is selected
**Solution:** Make sure **"Contiguous" is UNCHECKED** in the top toolbar. This will select ALL black areas, not just connected ones.

### **Problem:** It selected the text/shield too
**Solution:** Lower the **Tolerance** to 20-25 and try again. The text and shield are different colors (white, red, navy) so they shouldn't be selected if tolerance is low enough.

### **Problem:** Small black edges remain around text
**Solution:** After deleting the main background:
1. Zoom in (Ctrl + or Cmd +)
2. Use the **Eraser Tool** (E key) to manually erase the edges
3. OR select those areas with Magic Wand and delete again

### **Problem:** Can't see if background is transparent
**Solution:** The checkerboard pattern means transparent. To test:
1. Create a new layer (Layer → New Layer)
2. Drag it BELOW your logo layer
3. Fill it with a bright color (like green or pink)
4. If you can see that color behind your logo, it's transparent! ✅
