Course Overview

P2 - Marksmanship Basics builds on your pistol fundamentals with focused training on accuracy and consistency. This 2 hour course teaches proper trigger control, breath management, and follow-through techniques that transform shooters from capable to precise.

You'll develop a smooth trigger press, learn to diagnose and correct common shooting errors, and build repeatable accuracy through structured drills. These marksmanship fundamentals are the same techniques used by competitive shooters, military marksmen, and law enforcement professionals.

Whether you completed P1 and want to improve your accuracy or you're an experienced shooter looking to refine your technique, P2 provides the skills that separate adequate shooters from exceptional marksmen. Perfect for anyone who wants consistent, repeatable accuracy.

What Makes This Course Different

  • Precision Focus: Move beyond basic shooting to consistent accuracy
  • Error Diagnosis: Learn to identify and correct your own mistakes
  • Trigger Mastery: Develop a smooth, surprise-break trigger press
  • High Round Count: 150-200 rounds of focused practice
  • Measurable Results: Watch your groups tighten throughout the course

What You'll Learn

Detailed learning objectives for P1

Trigger Control & Press Techniques

Master the smooth, straight-to-the-rear trigger press that's essential for accuracy. Learn proper trigger finger placement, the surprise break technique, and how to press without disturbing sight alignment. Understand trigger reset and how to use it for faster follow-up shots.

Breath Control & Timing

Learn proper breathing techniques for shooting. Understand natural respiratory pause, when to take the shot, and how breath control affects accuracy. Practice coordinating breath with trigger press for consistent results.

Follow-Through & Recoil Management

Develop proper follow-through to maintain accuracy. Learn to manage recoil effectively, call your shots, and track sights through recoil. Practice recovery techniques for faster, more accurate follow-up shots.

Sight Alignment & Picture

Master front sight focus—the key to accurate pistol shooting. Learn proper sight alignment (equal height, equal light) and sight picture. Practice acquiring your sights quickly and maintaining focus throughout the trigger press. Understand sight radius and how it affects accuracy.

Loading, Unloading & Malfunction Clearing

Practice safe loading and unloading procedures until they become second nature. Learn to recognize common malfunctions (failure to fire, failure to eject, double feed). Master the tap-rack-bang immediate action drill for clearing stoppages safely and efficiently.

Live-Fire Fundamentals Practice

Apply everything you've learned with live-fire exercises at 7 yards. Focus on consistent accuracy through proper fundamentals. Shoot at a controlled pace, building confidence with each round. Leave with the skills to continue practicing safely on your own.

What You'll Practice

Hands-on drills and exercises in this course

Trigger Control Drills (100+ Repetitions)

Extensive dry-fire and live-fire trigger press drills. Focus on smooth, consistent press without disturbing sights.

Accuracy Drills at 7, 10, and 15 Yards

Progressive accuracy drills at increasing distances. Measure group size and work to tighten groups through proper technique.

Shot Calling & Self-Diagnosis (150+ Rounds)

Fire 150-200 rounds while learning to call your shots and diagnose errors. Understand what causes fliers and how to correct them.

Loading/Unloading Procedures (20+ Repetitions)

Practice loading magazines, chamber checks, safe unloading, and chamber verification until it's instinctive.

Basic Accuracy at 7 Yards

Establish your baseline accuracy at close range. Focus on fundamentals, not speed. Build confidence through consistent hits.

Malfunction Clearance (Dummy Round Practice)

Practice tap-rack-bang and other clearance drills with dummy rounds. Learn to clear stoppages safely without looking at the gun.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about P1

I've never shot a gun before. Is P1 really for me?

Absolutely! P1 is specifically designed for complete beginners. We start with the basics and build from there. Many of our best students started with zero experience. You'll receive individual attention in a supportive environment where questions are encouraged.

What kind of pistol should I bring?

Any reliable semi-automatic pistol works fine. We recommend 9mm, .40, or .45 caliber. Glock, Smith & Wesson, Sig Sauer, Springfield, and similar quality brands are all excellent choices. If you're not sure, contact us before the course and we'll help you choose.

Can I rent a firearm for this course?

Contact us before booking. Depending on the range we're using, rentals may be available. We can also recommend local shops where you can rent or purchase a firearm before the course. Many ranges require you to bring your own firearm for liability reasons.

What if I can only bring 50 rounds instead of 100?

While 100-150 rounds is recommended for maximum practice, you can still participate with 50 rounds. You'll get quality instruction on fundamentals—you'll just have fewer repetitions. We recommend bringing at least 75 rounds if possible to get the most value from the course.

Is this course required for my concealed carry permit?

P1 is not a concealed carry permit course—it's real firearms training. Many students take P1 before or after getting their permit to develop actual shooting skills beyond the permit requirements. Check Indiana's requirements for your specific permit needs.

How many students are in each class?

We limit P1 classes to a maximum of 8 students. This ensures everyone receives individual attention and coaching. You're not just a number in a crowded class—you get real instruction and feedback throughout the entire 2 hours.

What's your cancellation policy?

Cancel 48+ hours before your course for a full refund or free reschedule. Cancel 24-48 hours before for 50% refund. Less than 24 hours notice receives no refund (though we may offer credit at our discretion). See our full cancellation policy.
